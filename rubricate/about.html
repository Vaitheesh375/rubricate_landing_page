About Rubricate

We believe AI gets
better when real
experts are involved.

Rubricate was built on a simple conviction: the most capable AI systems are the ones shaped by people with genuine, deep, real-world expertise — not just large crowds clicking through tasks.

The gap between AI potential and AI performance is almost always a knowledge gap. We close that gap.
Our Mission

Making expertise
the foundation
of AI

The AI industry has a quality problem — and it's not a compute problem. It's a knowledge problem. The models that power medicine, law, finance, and science are being trained and evaluated by people who don't work in those fields.

Rubricate exists to fix that. We've built the infrastructure to connect genuine domain experts with the AI companies that need them — at scale, with rigor, and with the respect their expertise deserves.

We also believe that expertise is a two-way exchange. When doctors help train medical AI, when attorneys shape legal reasoning models, when scientists evaluate scientific claims — those experts learn something too. The interaction makes both the human and the AI sharper.

That's the world we're building: one where human intelligence and artificial intelligence push each other forward, guided by the people who know their fields most deeply.

Expertise deserves respect

We pay fairly for specialized knowledge and design our platform so expert time is never wasted on tasks beneath their ability.

Quality over quantity, always

A hundred careful expert annotations are worth more than ten thousand careless ones. We've built everything around this belief.

Transparency builds trust

We tell experts exactly what they're working on, why it matters, and how they'll be paid. We tell clients exactly how quality is measured and enforced.

AI should serve people

We're not neutral about the direction of AI development. We believe it should be shaped by humans who understand its real-world consequences.

Why the Name

A word with
deep roots

To rubricate means to mark or distinguish something in red — historically used by scribes to highlight the most important parts of a document, the sections that required the reader's attention and careful consideration.

In the age of AI, we see ourselves doing something similar: marking what matters. Identifying the places where human judgment is essential, where expert knowledge is irreplaceable, where a machine's answer isn't enough.

Rubricate is where you go to add the human signal that makes AI trustworthy. The red ink on the manuscript of intelligence.
